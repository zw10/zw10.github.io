The ‘no free lunch’ (NFL) theorem resembles the Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH) from part I in that their true values lies in exactly the opposite of what they say at face value. The folk version of the NFL says that that no algorithm or optimisation technique will perform any better for all cases. Effectively, that there is no silver bullet or ‘algorithmic arbitrage’ that will magically solve all your needs. This theorem is frequently used to justify further fine-tuning on models.
